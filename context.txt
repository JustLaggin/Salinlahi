The  distribution  of  government  assistance  known  as  “Ayuda”  is  a  very  important  service  
that’s
 
designed
 
in
 
order
 
to
 
support
 
affected
 
citizens
 
during
 
emergencies
 
and
 
economic
 
hardship.
 
However,
 
the
 
management
 
in
 
this
 
distribution
 
faces
 
immense
 
logistical
 
challenges.
 
To
 
address
 
this,
 
the
 
Salinlahi
 
Ayuda
 
Management
 
and
 
Distribution
 
App
 
is
 
a
 
proposed
 
centralized,
 
digital
 
platform
 
tailored
 
to
 
streamline
 
barangay-level
 
aid
 
distribution
 
through
 
direct,
 
offline-capable
 
QR
 
code
 
scanning
 
and
 
automated
 
attendance
 
tracking.
 
  Background/Project  Context  
The  Problem  Statement  
Currently,  the  Local  Government  Units  (LGUs)  of  Santo  Tomas,  Batangas,  manage  Ayuda  
distribution
 
using
 
a
 
heavily
 
manual
 
process
 
involving
 
paper-based
 
applications,
 
printed
 
spreadsheet
 
manifests,
 
and
 
physical
 
face-to-face
 
identity
 
validation.
 
This
 
traditional
 
approach
 
causes
 
massive
 
logistical
 
bottlenecks
 
and
 
operational
 
inefficiencies.
 
Specifically,  this  manual  system  causes  distinct  problems  for  different  users:  
●  For  Citizens  (Beneficiaries):  They  suffer  through  long  waiting  times  ranging  from  
30
 
minutes
 
to
 
1
 
hour
 
in
 
the
 
heat,
 
often
 
facing
 
confusion
 
due
 
to
 
lost
 
paperwork
 
or
 
a
 
lack
 
of
 
real-time
 
schedule
 
transparency.
 ●  For  Barangay  Distribution  Staff:  They  struggle  to  quickly  verify  identities  
manually,
 
leading
 
to
 
duplicate
 
claims,
 
missing
 
beneficiary
 
names,
 
and
 
highly
 
stressful
 
crowd-control
 
situations.
 ●  For  Barangay  Kapitans  (Administrators):  They  face  severe  difficulties  in  encoding  
data,
 
checking
 
participant
 
eligibility,
 
and
 
creating
 
events
 
due
 
to
 
the
 
lack
 
of
 
an
 
optimized
 
desktop
 
interface
 
for
 
complex
 
data
 
management.

Project  Rationale  
A  new  software  solution  is  required  to  directly  replace  the  manual  verification  queues  with  an  
automated,
 
offline-capable
 
digital
 
workflow.
 
Salinlahi
 
is

from
 
existing
 
apps
 
because

on

QR-centric
 
disbursement

By

LGU
 
can

the

spotty

at
 
distribution
 
sites

the
 
average
 
claiming

from
 
1
 
hour

to
 
under
 
5

ensuring
 
equitable
 
aid
 
distribution.

Scope  and  Limitations  
Scope  
The  boundaries  of  this  software  project  are  defined  by  the  following  parameters:  
●  Objectives:  To  deliver  a  working  MVP  for  rapid  QR-centric  aid  distribution  and  
attendance
 
tracking.
 ●  Features/Functionality:  User  registration,  generation  and  scanning  of  offline-capable  
QR
 
codes,
 
manual
 
ID
 
verification
 
fallback,
 
and
 
a
 
widescreen
 
administrative
 
dashboard.
 ●  Target  Population/Users:  Santo  Tomas  Citizens  (Beneficiaries),  Barangay  
Distribution
 
Staff,
 
and
 
Barangay
 
Kapitans.
 ●  Geographical  Area:  The  initial  implementation  and  testing  are  restricted  to  the  
barangays
 
within
 
Santo
 
Tomas,
 
Batangas.
 ●  Timeframe:  The  development,  data  gathering,  and  testing  phases  will  be  conducted  
from
 
January
 
2026
 
to
 
May
 
2026.

 ●  Resource/Time  Constraints:  Building  a  complex  approval  and  eligibility  screening

The

 ●  Geographical  Constraints:  The  app  will  only  be  deployed  at  Santo  Tomas,  Batangas
